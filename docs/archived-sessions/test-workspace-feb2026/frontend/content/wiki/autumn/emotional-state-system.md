<h1>Emotional State System</h1>
<p>The <strong>Emotional State System</strong> forms the core mechanical foundation of AUTUMN, transforming traditional resource management into sophisticated emotional and social energy tracking that drives all gameplay interactions. Unlike conventional energy systems focused on combat or crafting activities, AUTUMN&#39;s emotional framework emphasizes authentic human connection, personal boundaries, and the genuine costs of social interaction within family and community relationships. edit test</p>
<p>This system operates through <strong>social energy management</strong> where conversations, relationships, and emotional interactions consume finite emotional resources that require recovery through self-care activities including reading, creative expression, and solitude. The framework recognizes that meaningful social connection requires emotional investment while honoring the reality that social interaction can be draining even when positive and necessary.</p>
<p>The Emotional State System serves as AUTUMN&#39;s primary vehicle for exploring themes of introversion, family dynamics, and personal boundary management within loving but complex relationships. By making emotional energy visible and manageable, the system validates the player experience of social fatigue while providing tools for sustainable relationship building and community engagement.</p>
<h2>Social Energy Management Core</h2>
<h3>Base Social Meter Framework</h3>
<p><strong>Autumn&#39;s Energy Management</strong> operates through a <strong>100-point base social meter</strong> representing emotional and social energy available for meaningful interactions, creating visible feedback about emotional capacity while requiring strategic planning for social engagement. The meter provides clear boundaries around social interaction while enabling recovery through self-care activities.</p>
<p>Energy depletion occurs through conversation participation with <strong>costs ranging from 5-15 points</strong> depending on emotional intensity and relationship depth, ensuring that meaningful conversations require genuine emotional investment while preventing overwhelming social obligation. Cost variation reflects realistic differences between casual social interaction and deep emotional processing.</p>
<p><strong>Recovery methods</strong> provide diverse self-care options including reading (+20 points), decorating (+15 points), playing music (+25 points), and resting (+30 points), creating gameplay incentives for introspective activities while validating different approaches to emotional recovery and self-care.</p>
<p><strong>Low energy effects</strong> include reduced conversation options and negative mood states that affect other characters, creating social consequences for emotional neglect while encouraging sustainable interaction patterns. Energy effects provide feedback about emotional state without punishing players for necessary self-care.</p>
<h3>Dynamic Energy Cost Calculation</h3>
<p>Energy costs scale with <strong>emotional intensity and relationship depth</strong>, ensuring that casual interactions remain accessible while significant emotional processing requires appropriate energy investment. This scaling reflects realistic social dynamics where deep conversations demand more emotional resources than surface-level social exchange.</p>
<p><strong>Relationship depth factors</strong> affect energy costs through familiarity benefits that reduce interaction costs with close family members while maintaining energy requirements for meaningful emotional processing. Depth benefits reward relationship investment while acknowledging that even close relationships require emotional energy.</p>
<p><strong>Emotional intensity scaling</strong> increases costs for conversations involving conflict resolution, emotional support, or significant personal sharing, creating realistic resource requirements for difficult but necessary family interactions. Intensity scaling validates the emotional labor involved in maintaining healthy relationships.</p>
<p><strong>Context modifiers</strong> adjust energy costs based on timing, environment, and character emotional states, ensuring that social interaction costs reflect realistic factors affecting conversation difficulty and emotional demand.</p>
