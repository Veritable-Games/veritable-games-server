# Hackable Terminals

**Hackable Terminals** are interactive computer systems throughout ON COMMAND
environments that provide tactical information, environmental control, and
strategic advantages through
[[on-command-character-classes|specialist hacking abilities]].

## Terminal Types

### Information Terminals

**Primary sources of battlefield intelligence and tactical data:**

#### Strategic Intelligence

- **Enemy Position Data**: Real-time information on opponent locations and
  movements
- **Resource Locations**: Maps showing supply caches, equipment, and valuable
  materials
- **Environmental Data**: Information on terrain features, weather conditions,
  and hazards
- **Mission Parameters**: Updates on objectives, time constraints, and success
  conditions

#### Communication Networks

- **Command Structure**: Information on opponent command hierarchy and
  decision-making
- **Communication Intercepts**: Captured enemy communications and coordination
- **Allied Updates**: Information from friendly forces and support units
- **Emergency Channels**: Access to emergency communication and support systems

### Control Terminals

**Systems providing direct environmental and facility control:**

#### Environmental Systems

- **Atmosphere Control**: Managing air quality, temperature, and atmospheric
  pressure
- **Lighting Systems**: Controlling illumination for tactical advantage
- **Security Systems**: Managing locks, barriers, and access control
- **Power Management**: Controlling electrical systems and energy distribution

#### Facility Operations

- **Transportation Control**: Managing elevators, conveyor systems, and
  automated transport
- **Manufacturing Systems**: Controlling production facilities and automated
  equipment
- **Resource Distribution**: Managing supply systems and resource allocation
- **Emergency Systems**: Controlling fire suppression, medical systems, and
  evacuation procedures

### [[nanite-technology|Nanite Integration]] Terminals

**Advanced systems utilizing nanite technology for enhanced capabilities:**

#### Nanite Command

- **Nanite Deployment**: Controlling nanite swarms for construction and repair
- **Medical Nanites**: Directing medical nanite systems for injury treatment
- **Reconnaissance Nanites**: Controlling nanite scouts for intelligence
  gathering
- **Defensive Nanites**: Managing nanite-based defensive systems

#### Adaptive Systems

- **System Reconfiguration**: Using nanites to modify terminal capabilities in
  real-time
- **Self-Repair**: Nanite-enhanced terminals capable of maintaining function
  despite damage
- **Network Extension**: Nanites enabling temporary connection to otherwise
  isolated systems
- **Security Enhancement**: Nanite-based security measures protecting against
  unauthorized access

## Hacking Mechanics

### Skill Requirements

- **Technical Expertise**: Specialized knowledge required for successful
  terminal access
- **Time Investment**: Hacking requiring significant
  [[time-to-prepare-system|preparation time]] allocation
- **Risk Assessment**: Potential consequences of failed hacking attempts
- **Team Coordination**: Multiple specialists working together on complex
  systems

### Security Countermeasures

- **Access Restrictions**: Varying levels of security requiring different
  approaches
- **Detection Systems**: Terminal security that alerts opponents to hacking
  attempts
- **Countermeasure Activation**: Security systems that activate when
  unauthorized access is detected
- **Trace Protocols**: Systems that attempt to identify and locate hackers

### Success Outcomes

- **Information Acquisition**: Gaining valuable intelligence for tactical
  planning
- **System Control**: Obtaining direct control over environmental and facility
  systems
- **Access Expansion**: Opening new routes and options for squad movement
- **Advantage Creation**: Generating tactical advantages through system
  manipulation

## Integration with Squad Operations

### [[strategic-points-system|Strategic Points]] Enhancement

- **Point Control**: Terminal access enhancing control of strategic positions
- **Information Advantage**: Intelligence from terminals improving strategic
  point utilization
- **Environmental Control**: Using terminals to modify strategic point
  characteristics
- **Access Control**: Terminal hacking enabling or restricting access to
  strategic areas

### [[emplacement-zones-system|Emplacement Zones]] Support

- **Construction Assistance**: Terminal control providing resources for
  emplacement construction
- **Defensive Integration**: Connecting emplacement zones with facility
  defensive systems
- **Resource Access**: Terminal hacking providing materials for emplacement
  enhancement
- **Coordination Systems**: Using terminals to coordinate multiple emplacement
  zones

### Mission Coordination

- **Objective Updates**: Terminals providing real-time mission information
- **Team Communication**: Enhanced communication capabilities through terminal
  access
- **Resource Management**: Terminal control improving squad resource allocation
- **Emergency Support**: Terminal access providing emergency assistance and
  support

## Atlas Tether Integration

### City Systems Access

Within [[atlas-tether|Atlas Tether]] urban environments:

- **Municipal Systems**: Access to city-wide infrastructure and services
- **Transportation Networks**: Control over urban transportation and movement
  systems
- **Utility Management**: Managing power, water, and waste systems
- **Public Safety**: Access to emergency services and public safety systems

### Civilian Considerations

- **Population Safety**: Ensuring terminal access doesn't endanger civilian
  populations
- **Service Continuity**: Maintaining essential services during and after
  terminal access
- **Privacy Protection**: Respecting civilian privacy while accessing public
  systems
- **Infrastructure Preservation**: Avoiding damage to systems civilians depend
  on

## Non-Lethal Applications

### Humanitarian Uses

Consistent with ON COMMAND's non-lethal approach:

- **Medical System Access**: Using terminals to coordinate medical care for all
  casualties
- **Emergency Services**: Accessing emergency response systems for civilian
  protection
- **Communication Facilitation**: Enabling communication between separated
  groups
- **Resource Distribution**: Coordinating humanitarian aid and resource sharing

### Conflict De-escalation

- **Information Sharing**: Using terminals to share information that might
  prevent conflict
- **Communication Channels**: Opening diplomatic channels through terminal
  networks
- **Transparency**: Providing information that builds trust between opposing
  forces
- **Verification**: Using terminal data to verify agreements and reduce
  suspicion

## Countermeasures and Protection

### Terminal Security

- **Physical Protection**: Securing terminals against physical damage and
  interference
- **Electronic Security**: Protecting against unauthorized electronic access
- **Access Monitoring**: Tracking and recording all terminal access attempts
- **Backup Systems**: Maintaining redundant systems for critical functions

### Information Security

- **Data Encryption**: Protecting sensitive information stored in terminal
  systems
- **Access Control**: Limiting terminal access to authorized personnel only
- **Audit Trails**: Maintaining records of all terminal access and activity
- **Information Classification**: Organizing information based on security
  requirements

## Advanced Applications

### Predictive Analysis

- **Pattern Recognition**: Using terminal data to identify opponent patterns and
  predict behavior
- **Resource Prediction**: Analyzing data to predict resource needs and
  availability
- **Threat Assessment**: Using terminal information to assess and prepare for
  potential threats
- **Outcome Modeling**: Using terminal data to model potential mission outcomes

### Network Warfare

- **System Integration**: Connecting multiple terminals for enhanced
  capabilities
- **Network Control**: Gaining control over interconnected system networks
- **Information Warfare**: Using terminal access for information and
  psychological operations
- **System Disruption**: Disrupting opponent systems through terminal access
